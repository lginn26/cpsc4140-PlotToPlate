What is a Garden Plot?

A garden plot is a designated space within a community garden where you can grow your own plants, vegetables, or herbs.

Plot Types:
  • Available Plots - Regular growing spaces you can claim
  • Water Source - Designated water access points (tap, hose, irrigation)
  • Tool Hub - Storage area for shared gardening tools and equipment
Tip: Click on any plot in the garden grid to view its details and claim it if available!
Understanding Your Gardening Streak

Your gardening streak tracks consecutive days of activity in the FoodShare community.

How to Maintain Your Streak:
  • Post updates about your garden
  • Claim or tend to your garden plots
  • Share food with the community
  • Reply to community posts
  • Join or create gardens
Note: Your streak resets if you don't have any activity for 24 hours!

Following a Garden:

Stay updated on gardens you're interested in! Click the "Follow" button on any garden card to follow it. Your followed gardens will appear in your profile, and you can unfollow anytime by clicking the button again.

Garden Grid Colors:
  • Green - Available plots
  • Blue - Water source
  • Orange - Tool hub
  • Pink - Your claimed plots
  • Gold - Plots claimed by others
  • Gray - Unavailable areas

Community Posts
Creating Posts:

Share your harvest, ask questions, or offer surplus food to the community!

  • Click "New Post" in the Community section
  • Add a title, description, and optional image
  • Include food type, quantity, and location
  • Submit to share with the community
Interacting with Posts:
  • Like posts to show appreciation
  • Reply to start conversations
  • Mark as Resolved when your post is fulfilled (author only)
  • Delete your own posts if needed
Best Practices:
  • Be specific about food types and quantities
  • Include pickup location and timeframes
  • Respond to replies promptly
  • Mark posts as resolved when complete
